ABOUT US

SERDAL stands for the Socio-Economics Research and Data Analytics Laboratory, started as a two-year project funded by the Philippine Council for Agriculture, Aquatic, and Natural Resources Research and Development (PCAARRD) under the Department of Science and Technology (DOST). The project is implemented by the University of the Philippines Los Ba√±os, through the College of Economics and Management (CEM). The initiative aims to enhance the institutional research capacity of the R&D network in socio-economics.

SERDAL Phase 2, which began in March 2024 and will run for one year, focuses on institutionalizing the SERDAL Facility and establishing it as a self-sustaining entity within the university and the socio-economics field. The project team is committed to providing consultations and research software to support this initiative.

MISSION AND VISION

MISSION

To capacitate AANR socio-economic researchers in support of formulation and implementation of policies, programs, and other initiatives for socio-economic development.

VISION

To be the leading institution in providing technical support for socio-economic research and development for agriculture, aquatic and natural resources (AANR) sectors in the country.

OBJECTIVES

To enhance the services offered by SERDAL-UPLB, as the Agricultural Commodities Data Analytics and Forecasting Unit, through the development of new learning modules, utilizing different learning platforms, and hosting of databases from other partner institutions to enhance the research and innovation capacity of the country. Specifically, it aims to:

1. Develop a proposal for the creation of a non-degree course on future intelligence or foresight.

2. Enhance SERDAL‚Äôs repository functions by serving as the curator and mirror site for socio-economic and demographic data of other institutions.

3. Establish an open data system available for use of researchers and students in developing offshoot research projects.

4. Develop a mechanism for providing quick response advisories addressing pressing concerns.

5. Design new sets of learning modules in support of building the capacity on future intelligence gathering (foresight) and basic data science for agribusiness; economic research methods and modeling; data management using statistical tools; and data analysis, utilizing different learning platforms.

6. Build the capacity of SERDACs (main and satellite centers) on delivering the new training modules and in developing series of structured courses.

7. and Provide state-of-the-art facilities and equipment for the conduct of socio-economics research.
